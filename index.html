Big picture: HTML is the most basic building block of the Web. It's the pumpkin spice latte of programming languages - a real basic bitch. It defines the meaning and structure of web content. Imagine a description of a series a squares all over the page with different things in them. Google Chrome takes that description and renders it in the browser for you to see with your eyes. When you look at that description, you don't see the squares - you have to imagine them. It just looks like text with <'s' and /'s'.

CAN WE GET BACK TO WHAT THE LETTERS MEAN.

So starting with 'hypertext' (which apparently is one word but we're pretending it's two seperate words for our acrostic). Hypertext are the links that connect webpages together OR even connects a place on the webpage to another part of the page. They are the pieces of string connecting all the elements of your insane conspiracy theory about Pepe Silvia.

Markup language is a system that annotates the text in a way that looks different than the content. It's there to format the text, like instructions for your computer. It's like the crew of the movie - they're getting their actors and elements ready, but in the final product, the crew is hidden and you just see the elements. What a metaphor.

Ingredients for the perfect HTML page
  • One Document Type Declaration (HTML5 if you can find it)
  • Two dashes of the <html> tag
  • One <head> element, to really highlight all that metadata
  • One <body> element, to keep your content in-line (or block :) )
  • Close it all out with a <footer> element
  • Sprinkle some content on top for flavor **chef's kiss**

And voila, you have yourself a good old-fashioned web page.
